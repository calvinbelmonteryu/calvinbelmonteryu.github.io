San Diego Fishing Guide

San Diego offers some of the most diverse saltwater fishing on the entire West Coast. The city sits right on the Pacific and benefits from warm currents that bring in a huge variety of species throughout the year. In the summer months, the water heats up and yellowfin tuna, dorado, and bluefin tuna move within range of the sportfishing fleet. During the cooler months, calico bass, white seabass, and halibut become the primary targets for anglers fishing closer to shore. No matter the season, there is always something biting in San Diego.

One of the best things about fishing here is the accessibility. You do not need a boat to have a great day on the water. The Ocean Beach Pier and Shelter Island Pier are free to fish and regularly produce mackerel, bonito, and the occasional halibut. For a more adventurous experience, kayak fishing out of La Jolla Shores puts you right over the kelp beds where calico bass, yellowtail, and even white seabass patrol. If you want to go bigger, the Point Loma Sportfishing and H&M Landing docks run daily half-day, three-quarter-day, and full-day trips that take you anywhere from the local kelp beds out to the offshore banks where the tuna live.

My personal favorite setup for a casual day is surf fishing at Imperial Beach or Silver Strand. You only need a basic rod, some sand crabs or cut bait, and a folding chair. The corbina and surfperch fishing there can be surprisingly good, especially during the summer. It is a relaxing way to spend a morning and a good reminder that fishing does not always have to be about chasing the biggest fish — sometimes it is just about being outside and enjoying the coast.

Top Fishing Spots

  • Ocean Beach Pier — free pier fishing, great for mackerel and bonito
  • La Jolla Shores — kayak fishing over kelp beds for calico bass and yellowtail
  • Point Loma — sportfishing fleet with half-day and full-day offshore trips
  • Mission Bay — calm water, good for spotted bay bass and halibut
  • Imperial Beach — surf fishing for corbina and surfperch
  • Coronado Islands (via long-range boat) — yellowtail, tuna, and dorado

Recommended Gear for Beginners

If you are just getting started with fishing in San Diego, here is a basic gear list that will cover most situations:

  1. A 7-foot medium-action spinning rod and reel combo
  2. 20-pound braided line with a fluorocarbon leader
  3. A tackle box with assorted hooks, swivels, and sinkers
  4. Swimbaits and iron jigs for bass and yellowtail
  5. A California fishing license (required for anyone 16 and older)
  6. Sunscreen, polarized sunglasses, and a hat
